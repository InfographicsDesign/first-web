Chicken Biryani

Biryani is a classic Indian mixed rice dish that is part of the Indian Mughlai culinary tradition, which traces its roots to central Asian cuisine and a rich fusion with Indian flavors and techniques.
It is prepared with layers of fluffy basmati rice, marinated meat or chicken, browned onions, cilantro, mint, yogurt, saffron, ghee and spices. Classic biryani can justly be called the crown jewel of Indian cuisine, a dream dish that should not be missed.

Gathering Ingredients

Fried Onions

Ingredients Quantity
Onions [Finely Sliced] 3/4 lb
Peanut Oil 1 and 1/2 Cups

Chicken Marination

Ingredients Quantity
Chicken [Cut and Clean] 1 and 1/2 lb
Plain Yogurt 2/3 Cups
Ginger Garlic Paste 1 and 1/2 Tablespoon
Salt [Adjust per Taste] 1 Tablespoon
Chilli Powder 1 Tablespoon
Turmeric [Ground] 1/2 Tablespoon

Spices

Ingredients Quantity
Cinnamon Stick 1 pc
Black Cardamom 1 pc
Green Cardamom 5 pc
Cloves 5 pc
Bay Leaves [Large] 1 pc
Shah Jeera 1 Tablespoon
Black Pepper 1/2 Tablespoon

Biryani

Ingredients Quantity
Basmati Rice [Long Grain] 2 Cups
Salt [Adjust per Taste] 1 Tablespoon
Olive Oil 2 Tablespoon
Lemon Juice 2 Tablespoon
Cilantro [Fresh, Chopped] 1/4 Cup
Mint [Fresh, Chopped] 2 Tablespoon
Saffron [Ground]{Dissolved in 2 Tablespoon Hot Water} 1 Tablespoon
Ghee (*Optional) 2 Tablespoon

Whole Spices for Rice

Ingredients Quantity
Black Cardamom 1 pc
Green Cardamom 3 pc
Clove 4 pc
Bay Leaves 2 pc
Cinnamon Stick 1/2 pc

Equipment

  • Rice Cooker
  • Wide Frying Pan
  • Spice Grinder
  • Oven Proof Cooking Pot

Instructions for Cooking

Fried Onions

  1. Heat the oil at medium high in a wide frying pan. If you place your palm about 3 inches over the surface of oil, you should feel the heat.
  2. Fry onions in batches. Do not crowd the pan. Fry until the color changes to crisp light golden brown. Set the pan aside.

Chicken Marination

  1. Combine chicken with all the marinade ingredients (Refer to the table) and 1/3 of fried onions.
    TIP: Add yogurt, starting with ½ cup and increase as needed to ¾ cup. The mixture should be thick and not runny.
  2. Grind the biryani spices in a spice grinder. Add them to the chicken marinade and combine. Marinate for about 3 to 5 hours.

Rice Preparation with Rice Cooker

  1. Rinse rice 4 to 5 times in lots of water until it is clear. Drain the rice well. Soak 1/3 cup basmati rice in water for one hour. Save the rest.
  2. Bring 2 and 1/4 cups of water to a rolling boil in a rice cooker. Add 3/4 teaspoon salt, 1 and 1/2 tablespoons olive oil and whole spices. Then, add the remaining 1 and 2/3 cups of basmati rice and stir. Let it cook until all the water is absorbed.
  3. It is important to NOT let the rice steam until it is fully done. Just when all the water is well absorbed, transfer cooked rice immediately to another wide open pan. In the rice cooker the whole process takes about 15 to 20 minutes.

Assembling Rice and Chicken Layers

  1. Transfer chicken with marinade to a 4 to 5 quart wide thick bottomed oven proof cooking pot (9 to 10 inch diameter). Make sure there is not too much marinade around chicken pieces and that it is not runny.
  2. Scatter cilantro, mint, half the lemon juice and ½ tablespoon olive oil over the chicken marinade. Then scatter most of the fried onions.
  3. Drain the soaked basmati rice very well and spread it over the fried onion layer. (This raw soaked rice will get cooked with the juices from chicken).
  4. Next, layer all the cooked rice. Top with remaining lemon juice, saffron water, fried onions and ghee.
    TIP: If you prefer, you may pick out the whole spices from cooked rice before steaming.

Final Steaming

  1. Place one sheet of parchment paper, followed by one or two sheets of aluminum foil over the cooking pot. Place the lid tight over it. Bake the biryani in a preheated oven at 350ºF for one hour, until steam builds up well.
  2. Alternatively, it can be cooked on a stove in a thick bottomed cooking pot. Heat the biryani at medium high for about 10 to 15 minutes and then reduce to low and cook for one hour until steam builds up well.
  3. Let it cool five minutes and gently mix rice and chicken. Using a spatula mound the biryani on a serving plate, making sure to present the rice in various colors - white, yellow, orange and brown. Place some chicken pieces on the top. Scatter browned onions, chopped herbs and extra saffron rice on top.
  4. Serve chicken biryani with Hyderabadi tomato egg curry or raita prepared with creamy yogurt, chopped red onions, deseeded jalapenos and cilantro.

Palak Paneer

Palak is spinach and Paneer is Indian cottage cheese, and the combination of both in a delicious gravy is called Palak Paneer.
It is a Punjabi bright green curry with blanched and pureed spinach as its base and paneer cubes simmered in it along with a few spices.

Gathering Ingredients

Ingredients Quantity
Spinach 600 g
Baking Soda 3/4 Tablespoon
Green Chilli 8 pc
Vegetable Oil 12 Tablespoon
Cumin Seeds 3 Tablespoon
Dry Chillies 7 pc
Onion [Finely Chopped] 3 Cups
Cloves & Garlic [Crushed] 15 pc
Tomato [Finely Chopped] 3 Cup
Coriander Powder 6 Tablespoon
Garam Masala Powder 1 and 1/2 Tablespoon
Salt [Adjust per Taste] 2 Tablespoon
Lemon Juice 6 Tablespoon
Fresh Cream 6 Tablespoon
Paneer 600 g

Instructions for Cooking

  1. Heat water in a large pot.
  2. Clean the spinach and wash it thoroughly under running water.
  3. Once the water comes to a boil, add the cooking soda and spinach and blanch the spinach for 3-4 minutes.
  4. Drain the water and run the spinach under cold water immediately.
  5. Put the spinach a blender along with green chillies and make a smooth paste.
  6. Heat oil in a pan.
  7. Once the oil is hot, add cumin seeds and dried red chillies.
  8. Let them crackle for a few seconds.
  9. Add onion and garlic and fry until onion turns translucent.
  10. Add tomato and cook for 3-4 minutes. You can blend the masala in a blender at this stage for a creamier gravy.
  11. Now add coriander powder, garam masala powder and fry for another minute.
  12. Add spinach puree and saute on high heat for minute.
  13. Add paneer, salt and 1/2 cup water and bring the curry to a boil.
  14. Switch off the heat and add lemon juice and mix well.
  15. For the Dhungar, heat a coal piece until it is red hot. Keep a bowl inside the bowl with curry and keep the piece of coal on top of it. Pour a tsp of ghee on top of the coal and immediately close the lid of the main bowl.
  16. Let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  17. Open the lid, remove the coal piece and the small bowl and give the curry a gentle mix.
  18. Garnish the Palak Paneer with Fresh Cream.
  19. Serve hot with Naan or Phulke.

Fish Curry

Fish Curry is an easy-to-make non-vegetarian recipe that you can prepare for your loved ones in lunch or dinner. This Mughlai recipe is prepared using cod fish, lemon juice and blended well with a melange of ground aromatic spices.
This fish recipe can be made using a few simple steps and is best for occasions like kitty parties and get-togethers. This non-vegetarian recipe tastes best when served with steamed rice or paratha. In short, it is a must try dish for all the non-vegetarians or sea food lovers out there!

Gathering Ingredients

Ingredients Quantity
Cod Fish 300 g
Lemon Juice 1 Teaspoon
Turmeric 1 Teaspoon
Yogurt 3 Tablespoon
Milk 1 Cup
Garam Masala Powder 1 Tablespoon
Salt [Adjust per Taste] 4 Tablespoon
Vegetable Oil 1/2 Cup
Poppy Seeds 3 Teaspoon
Onion 2 pc
Ginger 2 Inches
Cloves & Garlic 3 pc
Calcium 327 mg
Peppercorns 1 Tablespoon
Coriander Seeds 2 Teaspoon
Cumin Seeds 2 Teaspoon
Red Chilli Powder 2 Teaspoon
Almonds 8 pc
Vinegar 1 Tablespoon

Instructions for Cooking

  1. Wash the fish thoroughly and marinate with turmeric powder, lemon juice and salt for a minimum of 40 minutes.
  2. Boil the poppy seeds with enough water over medium flame to make them soft.
  3. Blend them chopped onions, garlic cloves, ginger, peppercorns, coriander seeds, cumin seeds, red chili powder, turmeric powder, almonds and salt in a blender jar over high speed for a few seconds till you achieve a smooth paste.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium flame and once the oil is hot enough, add the marinated fish pieces to it one by one.
  5. Fry for a minute and then in the same pan add ground onion paste. Saute till the raw smell disappears.
  6. Now, add the whisked curd along with garam masala powder and stir well.
  7. Add milk over it and cook fish in the gravy for some time over low flame. Turn off the gas knob when you achieve a thick gravy.
  8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or pulao.

What are Square Roots?

Square root of a number is a value, which on multiplied by itself gives the original number.
Suppose, x is the square root of y, then it is represented as x=√y. We can express the same equation as x^2 = y. Here,"√"is the radical symbol used to represent the root of numbers.
For example, the square of 3 is 9, 32 = 9 and the square root of 9, √9 = 3. Since 9 is a perfect square, hence it is easy to find the square root of such numbers, but for an imperfect square like 3, 7, 5, etc., it is really tricky to find the root.

Properties of Square Roots

In mathematics, a square root function is defined as a one-to-one function that takes a positive number as an input and returns the square root of the given input number.
f(x) = √x

For example, if x = 9, then the function returns the output value as 3. Some of the important properties of the square root are as follows:

  • If a number is a perfect square number, then there exists a perfect square root. If a number ends with an even number of zeros (0’s), then it can have a square root.
  • The two square root values can be multiplied. For example, √3 can be multiplied by √2, then the result should be √6.
  • When two same square roots are multiplied, then the result should be a radical number. It means that the result is a non-square root number. For instance, when √7 is multiplied by √7, the result obtained is 7.
  • The square root of any negative numbers is not defined. Because the perfect square cannot be negative.
  • If a number ends with 2, 3, 7 or 8 (in the unit digit), then the perfect square root does not exist.
  • If a number ends with 1, 4, 5, 6 or 9 in the unit digit, then the number will have a square root.

How to find Square Roots?

[Perfect Squares]

To find the square root of any perfect number (Eg. 4, 9, 16), we can factorise the number by prime factorisation method to find the square root of that number.
Example:
Square of 7 = 49
The square root of 49 = 7

[Not Perfect Squares]

To find the square root by long division method for imperfect squares
Example:
Square root of 436.

Thus, the square root of 436 is 20.880 (rounding to 3 decimals).

What are Cube Roots?

Cube root of number is a value which when multiplied by itself thrice or three times produces the original value. For example, the cube root of 27, denoted as Cubic Root of 27, is 3, because when we multiply 3 by itself three times we get 3 x 3 x 3 = 27.
So, we can say, the cube root gives the value which is basically cubed. Here, 27 is said to be a perfect cube. From the word, cube root, we can understand what is the root of the cube. It means which number caused the cube present under the root.
Usually, to find the cubic root of perfect cubes, we use the prime factorisation method. In a similar manner, we can learn the significance of square root here.

How to find Cube Root?

To find the cubic root of a number easily, we can use the prime factorisation method. By evaluating the prime factors we can pair similar digits in a group of three and take them out as a single digit from the cubic root.
Let us take an example of finding the cube root of 8. By factoring 8, we get; 8 = 2 x 2 x 2.
Now, if we take the cubic root both the sides, then the cube of 2 cancels the cubic root. Hence, we get the value of Cubic Root of 8. Therefore, Cubic Root of 8 = 2

Probability

What is Empirical Probability?

Experimental probability is defined as the number of trials where E has occurred for the total number of trials.
Experimental probability is also known as empirical probability.

How to find Probability?

An event of an experiment is defined as the collection of conclusions of experiments.

  • The probability of a certain event is always 1.
    Eg. In the month of monsoon, chances that it will rain that day is 1

  • The probability of an uncertain event is always 0.
    Eg. In a die, chances that you will get a 7 is 0

  • The Probaility of an event lies between 0 and 1.
    Eg. P(Of getting a 6 on a die) = 1/6 = 0.167

A certain event is also known as a sure event.

P (E)+P (Not E) = 1

P (E)= (Number of Trials in which E has happened)/(Total Number of Trials)

Volume

Volume is the quantity of three-dimensional space enclosed by a closed surface. For example, the space that a substance (Solid, Liquid, Gas) or shape occupies or contains. Volume is often quantified numerically using the SI derived unit, the cubic metre (cm^3).
The volume of a container is generally understood to be the capacity of the container; i. e., the amount of fluid (gas or liquid) that the container could hold, rather than the amount of space the container itself displaces.
Three dimensional mathematical shapes are also assigned volumes. Volumes of some simple shapes, such as regular, straight-edged, and circular shapes can be easily calculated using arithmetic formulas.

Narendra Modi

Prime Minister of India (2014-Present)

How he became the PM of India?

After the Bharatiya Janata Party led National Democratic Alliance won a landslide in the 2014 Lok Sabha election, Narendra Modi was sworn in as the Prime Minister of India on 26 May 2014.
He became the first Prime Minister born after India's independence from the British Empire.
Modi started his second term after the Bharatiya Janata Party led National Democratic Alliance won again in the 2019 Lok Sabha election.
Modi became the 4th longest serving Prime Minister of India and the longest serving Non-Congress Prime Minister in 2020.

His background?

Narendra Modi was born on 17 September 1950 to a Gujarati Hindu family of grocers in Vadnagar, Mehsana district, Bombay State (present-day Gujarat).
He was the third of six children born to Damodardas Mulchand Modi and Hiraben Mod
